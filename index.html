Understanding SEBI Guidelines for Investment Advisors

Introduction

Investment advisory services in India are governed by the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI). This blog post delves into the key aspects of the engagement between a SEBI-registered investment advisor and their client, focusing on compliance, fees, and client-advisor obligations.

Key Features of the Engagement

1. SEBI Registration and Compliance

The investment advisor is registered with SEBI under the Investment Advisers Regulations, 2013. This ensures that the advisor adheres to all legal requirements, maintaining a professional standard while providing financial advice.

2. Scope of Services

The advisor offers various services, such as financial planning, goal-based investment strategies, risk assessment, and portfolio recommendations, tailored to meet the client's needs and objectives.

3. Fee Structure

SEBI places a cap on the fees that advisors can charge, ensuring transparency and fairness:

  • The maximum fee is 2.5% of Assets Under Advice (AUA) per year.
  • Alternatively, a fixed fee of INR 1,25,000 annually can be charged.

This is as per Regulation 15A of SEBI's Investment Adviser regulations.

4. Client's Role

Clients must provide complete and accurate financial information, participate in the risk profiling process, and follow written investment advice as provided by the advisor.

5. Conflict of Interest & Confidentiality

Advisors must disclose any conflicts of interest as they arise and ensure that client information is kept confidential. This maintains a high level of trust and transparency in the client-advisor relationship.

6. Termination and Liability

Either party may terminate the engagement with a 30-day notice period. The advisor is not liable for any market losses or underperformance of investments, as long as they have adhered to SEBI regulations.

Relevant SEBI Regulations

AI Prompt: "What SEBI regulations apply to investment advisory services?"

Here are the key regulations:
  • SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013: Governs registration, duties, and code of conduct for investment advisers.
  • Regulation 19(1)(d): Pertains to the engagement terms between the investment adviser and client.
  • Regulation 15A: Establishes the maximum permissible fee caps for investment advisory services.
  • Code of Conduct (Third Schedule): Advisers must act in a fiduciary capacity and avoid conflicts of interest.
  • SEBI Circular (September 23, 2020): Updates guidelines on the fee structure for investment advisers.
